History

North America has a rich history that began with Indigenous peoples who lived on the continent for thousands of years before European explorers arrived. During the 15th and 16th centuries, Spain, France, and Britain established colonies. Over time, countries such as the United States, Canada, and Mexico gained independence. Today, North America is one of the world's most developed and economically important continents.

Culture

North America has a diverse culture shaped by Indigenous traditions, European settlement, African heritage, and immigration from around the world. The continent is known for its music, films, sports, literature, technology, and multicultural societies. Popular sports include basketball, baseball, American football, ice hockey, and soccer.

Religion

Christianity is the largest religion in North America, including Roman Catholicism and Protestantism. Islam, Judaism, Hinduism, Buddhism, Sikhism, and Indigenous spiritual traditions are also practiced. Most countries on the continent guarantee freedom of religion.

Population

North America has a population of about 600 million people. The largest countries by population are the United States, Mexico, and Canada. Major cities include New York City, Mexico City, Toronto, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Montreal.

Income

North America has one of the world's largest and most advanced economies. The United States has the largest economy on the continent, while Canada and Mexico are also major economic powers. Important industries include technology, manufacturing, finance, agriculture, tourism, energy, and international trade. Living standards are generally high, although income levels vary across countries and regions.

Political Systems

  • North America includes federal republics, constitutional monarchies, and presidential republics.

  • The United States and Mexico are federal presidential republics.

  • Canada is a federal parliamentary democracy and constitutional monarchy.

  • Many Caribbean nations are parliamentary democracies or constitutional monarchies.

  • Countries cooperate through organizations such as the United States–Mexico–Canada Agreement (USMCA) and the Organization of American States (OAS).

  • North America consists of 23 independent countries along with several territories and dependencies.

Languages

English, Spanish, and French are the most widely spoken languages in North America. English is the primary language in the United States and much of Canada, French is widely spoken in Quebec, and Spanish is the main language in Mexico. Hundreds of Indigenous languages are also spoken across the continent.
