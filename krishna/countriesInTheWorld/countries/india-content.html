History

India has one of the world's oldest civilizations, dating back to the Indus Valley Civilization around 2500 BCE. Over centuries, it witnessed the rise of powerful empires such as the Maurya, Gupta, Mughal, and later the British Raj. India gained independence from British rule in 1947 after a long non-violent struggle led by Mahatma Gandhi and other freedom fighters.

Culture

Indian culture is incredibly diverse and shaped by thousands of years of tradition. It is known for its classical music, dance forms (like Bharatanatyam, Kathak, and Odissi), art, festivals (Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas), and colorful clothing like sarees and turbans. Respect for elders, family values, and hospitality are important aspects of Indian culture.

religion

India is the birthplace of major world religions like Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism. It also has a large population of Muslims, Christians, Jews, and Zoroastrians, making it one of the most religiously diverse countries. Religious tolerance and co-existence have been a central part of India's social fabric.

population

India's population is to touch 1.7 billion in 2025, which is the highest in the world.

Income

India is the 4th largest economy in the world by nominal GDP (as of 2025). The GDP is around $4.187 trillion, with major sectors including IT, agriculture, manufacturing, and services. However, income inequality exists, with a mix of very high and very low incomes.

Parliamentary System

  • India follows a parliamentary democratic republic based on the Westminster model. It has

  • President (head of state)

  • Prime Minister (head of government)

  • Lok Sabha (Lower House) and Rajya Sabha (Upper House)
  • Lok sabha has 543 seats.

  • The Rajya Sabha, India's upper house of Parliament, has up to 250 members - 238 elected from States and Union Territories and 12 nominated by the President

  • The Prime Minister is the leader of the majority party in the Lok Sabha. India has a federal structure with 29 states and 8 Union Territories, each with its own government.

  • The term of the Lok Sabha is 5 years, unless dissolved earlier.
