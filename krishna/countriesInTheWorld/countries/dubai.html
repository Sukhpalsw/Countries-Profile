History

Dubai was originally a small fishing and trading village. It gained importance in the 19th century as a center for pearling and maritime trade. In 1971, Dubai became part of the United Arab Emirates (UAE). Since then, especially from the late 20th century onward, Dubai has rapidly grown into a global business and tourism hub.

Culture

Dubai’s culture blends traditional Arab heritage with modern global influences. Islamic traditions shape social life, but the city is home to over 200 nationalities, resulting in a highly multicultural environment. Hospitality, modesty, and respect are key cultural values.

Religion

The official religion is Islam. Mosques are widespread, and Islamic practices like prayer and Ramadan are followed strictly. However, Dubai allows religious freedom, and there are churches, Hindu temples, and other places of worship for its large expatriate population.

Foods

* Emirati Dishes: Shawarma, Kabsa, Harees, Machboos *

* International: Indian biryani, Filipino BBQ, Lebanese mezze, Italian pasta *

* Sweets: Luqaimat (fried sweet dumplings), dates, baklava *

Population

Dubai’s population is around 3.6 million (as of 2024), with only about 15% Emirati citizens. The rest are expatriates from countries such as India, Pakistan, the Philippines, and many others, making Dubai one of the most multicultural cities in the world.

Income

Dubai is a major contributor to the UAE’s GDP, which is over $500 billion. The economy is powered by tourism, real estate, trade, and finance. Dubai has a high per capita income and a very business-friendly environment.

Government System

  • Dubai is one of the seven emirates of the United Arab Emirates (UAE).

  • It is ruled by Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, who is also the Vice President and Prime Minister of the UAE.

  • The UAE is a federal absolute monarchy, and Dubai follows a monarchy-based government system.

  • The UAE has a Federal National Council with advisory powers, but Dubai’s leadership has strong central authority.

  • There are no political parties or democratic elections like in a parliamentary system.
