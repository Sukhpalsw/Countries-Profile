History

Australia,s history begins with its Indigenous peoples, the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples, who have lived on the continent for over 65,000 years. British colonization started in 1788 with the arrival of the First Fleet in Sydney. The country developed as six colonies, which united in 1901 to form the Commonwealth of Australia. Australia fought in both World Wars and has since grown into a prosperous and multicultural nation.

Culture

Australian culture is shaped by Indigenous heritage, British traditions, and multicultural immigration. It is known for its love of sports (cricket, rugby, surfing), outdoor lifestyle, and art. Aboriginal art, the "mateship" spirit, and national events like Australia Day and ANZAC Day are important parts of its cultural identity.

Religion

Australia is a secular country with freedom of religion. Christianity is the largest faith, followed by Islam, Buddhism, Hinduism, and Sikhism. A significant portion of the population identifies as having no religion.

Population

Australia has a population of about 26.7 million people (2024). The majority live in coastal cities like Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, and Perth. It is a multicultural society with immigrants from Europe, Asia, and other regions.

Income

Australia has a strong and stable economy, with a GDP of around $1.7 trillion (2024). Major industries include mining, agriculture, tourism, education, and financial services. It has a high standard of living and ranks among the top countries in quality-of-life indexes.

Government System

  • Australia is a federal parliamentary constitutional monarchy.

  • The British monarch is the ceremonial head of state, represented by the Governor-General.

  • The Prime Minister is the head of government and is elected by the majority party in the House of Representatives.
