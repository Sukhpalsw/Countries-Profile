History

Switzerlands history is rooted in ancient Celtic tribes and later Roman conquest. In 1291, several regions formed a confederation for mutual defense, marking the origin of modern Switzerland. The country maintained neutrality through both World Wars. Its long-standing tradition of neutrality and diplomacy has made it a hub for peace talks and international organizations.

Culture

Swiss culture reflects a mix of German, French, Italian, and Romansh influences. Each region (or canton) maintains distinct traditions, languages, and customs. Switzerland is known for punctuality, cleanliness, efficiency, and a high quality of life. Festivals, yodeling, Alpine traditions, and chocolate-making are key cultural elements.

Religion

Christianity is the major religion, with Roman Catholicism and Protestantism being the two largest denominations. There are also small Muslim, Jewish, and non-religious communities. Religious freedom is protected, and the society is largely tolerant and secular in governance.

    • Population

    Switzerland has a population of around 8.9 million (2024). The country is ethnically and linguistically diverse with four official languages: German, French, Italian, and Romansh. It also has a high number of international residents due to its global workforce and quality of life.

    Income

    Switzerland has one of the highest per capita incomes in the world. Its GDP is around $950 billion (2024). Major economic sectors include banking, finance, pharmaceuticals, watchmaking, tourism, and high-tech manufacturing. It has low unemployment and a strong currency (Swiss Franc).

    Government System

    • Switzerland is a federal parliamentary republic with a unique system of direct democracy.

    • The Federal Council (7 members) acts as the collective executive head of state and government.

    • There is no single president with executive power; instead, the council rotates its ceremonial presidency annually.

    • Parliament is bicameral: the National Council (lower house) and Council of States (upper house).

    • Citizens can propose referendums and vote directly on laws and policies.

    • Switzerland has 26 cantons, each with its own constitution, parliament, and government.
