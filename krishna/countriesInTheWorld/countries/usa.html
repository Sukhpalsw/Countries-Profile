History

The USA was founded in 1776 after declaring independence from British colonial rule. The American Revolution (1775–1783) led to the creation of a new democratic republic. Key historical events include:

  • Civil War (1861–1865) over slavery
  • Industrial Revolution
  • Participation in World Wars I & II

Culture

American culture is influenced by a mix of immigrant traditions, especially from Europe, Africa, Latin America, and Asia. The U.S. is a cultural powerhouse in:

  • Music (jazz, rock, hip-hop)
  • Hollywood film industry
  • Fashion, sports (NBA, NFL), fast food, and tech innovation

Religion

  • Christianity (largest group)
  • Judaism, Islam, Buddhism, Hinduism
  • A growing number of people identify as non-religious or atheist

Food

  • Burgers, hot dogs, and fried chicken
  • Pizza, tacos, and barbecue
  • Apple pie and doughnuts

Population

As of 2024, the USA has a population of about 335 million people, making it the third most populous country after India and China. It is ethnically and culturally diverse, with large immigrant communities.

Incomes abd GDP

The U.S. has the largest economy in the world, with a GDP of over $26 trillion (as of 2024). Key sectors include:

  • Technology, finance, healthcare, defense, and energ

Parliamentary System

  • President: Head of state and head of government, elected every 4 years.
  • Senate (100 members – 2 from each state)
  • House of Representatives (435 members)
  • Executive (President)
  • Legislative (Congress)
  • Judicial (Supreme Court)
