History

New Zealand's history begins with the Māori, the indigenous Polynesian people who arrived around the 13th century. In 1642, Dutch explorer Abel Tasman was the first European to reach New Zealand, followed by British explorer Captain James Cook in 1769. The Treaty of Waitangi was signed in 1840 between the British Crown and Māori chiefs, making New Zealand a British colony. It gained full independence in 1947 and is now a sovereign nation within the Commonwealth.

Culture

New Zealand has a unique blend of Māori and European (Pākehā) cultures, with growing influences from Pacific and Asian communities. Māori traditions such as the haka, carving, and weaving remain central to national identity. Kiwis are known for their friendliness, love of sports (especially rugby), and appreciation of nature and outdoor activities.

Religion

New Zealand is a secular nation with freedom of religion. Christianity is the largest religion, but many identify as non-religious. Other practiced religions include Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam, and Sikhism due to immigration.

Foods

* Seafood like green-lipped mussels and crayfish *

* Kiwi fruit and Manuka honey are also iconic *

Population

New Zealand has a population of about 5.2 million (2024). The largest city is Auckland, while Wellington is the capital. Māori make up about 17% of the population, alongside people of European, Pacific Island, and Asian heritage.

Income

New Zealand has a GDP of around $300 billion (2024). The economy is based on agriculture (dairy, meat, wool), tourism, forestry, film production, and technology. It has one of the highest standards of living and a strong export-based economy.

Government System

  • New Zealand is a parliamentary democracy and a constitutional monarchy.

  • The British monarch is the head of state, represented by the Governor-General.

  • The Prime Minister is the head of government.

  • It has a unicameral legislature called the House of Representatives.

  • New Zealand uses a Mixed-Member Proportional (MMP) electoral system, encouraging multi-party representation.

  • General elections are held every 3 years.
