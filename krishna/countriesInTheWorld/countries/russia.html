History

Russia’s history stretches back to the early East Slavic tribes and the formation of Kievan Rus in the 9th century. After the fall of Kiev, power shifted to Moscow, which grew into the Tsardom of Russia. In 1917, the Russian Empire collapsed during the revolution, leading to the creation of the Soviet Union in 1922. The USSR became a global superpower until its dissolution in 1991, after which the Russian Federation was formed. Russia has played major roles in world politics, science, and culture.

Culture

Russian culture is known for its literature, ballet, classical music, and fine arts. Writers like Tolstoy, Dostoevsky, and Chekhov shaped global literature. Ballet is a national treasure with world-famous schools and theaters. Russian traditions include folk dances, wooden crafts, Orthodox festivals, and strong family values.

Religion

The main religion in Russia is Russian Orthodox Christianity. Other practiced religions include Islam, Buddhism, Judaism, and various Christian denominations. Russia guarantees freedom of religion, although the Orthodox Church has strong cultural influence.

Population

Russia has a population of about 145 million people. It is the largest country in the world by land area, spanning Eastern Europe and Northern Asia. Major cities include Moscow, Saint Petersburg, Novosibirsk, and Yekaterinburg.

Income

Russia’s economy is one of the largest in the world, with a GDP of over $2 trillion. Key sectors include oil and natural gas, mining, manufacturing, agriculture, and defense industries. Income levels vary widely between big cities and rural regions.

Government System

  • Russia is a federal semi-presidential republic.

  • The President is the head of state and holds significant executive power.

  • The Prime Minister is the head of government and oversees administration.

  • The Federal Assembly is the parliament, made up of the State Duma (lower house) and the Federation Council (upper house).

  • Russia is divided into regions including republics, oblasts, and territories.

  • Presidential elections are held every 6 years.
